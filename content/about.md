**A United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (UK) Perspective. Part of a self-directed [learning project](https://www.modernmoney.studio).**

## Model Agents

A [model](https://www.modernmoney.studio/models#abmlp) consumes real-world UK economic time-series data beginning Q1 1974 to the latest available. A model run may go beyond the latest available time-series. To this end, [development]({{< ref "/development-roadmap" >}} "A Development Roadmap") explores agent decision-making, specifically the logic of government, central bank, and household(s) with respect to *expenditures*, *interest rate* policy and *asset portfolio* decisions respectively.

### Supposition

Long-term [Gilt](#gilt) dynamics are principally lagged effects of the new money bills created by a Government money system bound by [parameter]({{< ref "/development-roadmap/#model-parameter-rules" >}} "A Development Roadmap") rules tuned to the requirements of a particular [macro-financial](https://www.data-reports.net/studio-sketch/public-private-finance.html) regime.

{{< info-alert title="Gilt" id="gilt" >}}
A gilt is a UK Government liability denominated in sterling, issued by the UK Treasury and listed on the London Stock Exchange. The term *gilt* or *gilt-edged security* is a reference to the primary characteristic of gilts as an investment: their security.
{{< /info-alert >}}

### Project

To develop, analyse and learn from simple portfolio hedging strategies. In context, model household agent(s) have bond price expectations. All household agent(s) fall into group 1. A number of preferred household agent(s) fall into group 2.

1. Have access to Central Bank guidance;
2. In addition to Central Bank guidance, group 2 may access model system velocity and acceleration information.

Does model group 2 have an asset portfolio decision-making advantage?

### Model Variables

#### Dependent

- Macro: Bond yield and price.
- Micro: Household agent(s) and their long-bond price expectations.

#### Independent

- Government expenditure.
- Mutually exclusive Government taxation rate themes:
    - One flat rate of taxation for all household agents.
    - Marginal rate taxation brackets (household agent wage and system average wage).
    - Marginal rate taxation brackets (household agent wealth and type).
- The Central Bank sets a rate on interest-bearing money types.
