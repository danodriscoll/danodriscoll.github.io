## First

My interest in the history of the English monetary system eventually met with a reason to learn about the UK Government bond market. There's a longer story and reading list on my personal [site](https://www.danielodriscoll.me.uk/). In short, I wanted to better understand UK Government bonds. My goal was to build an analysis framework from which to test some simple strategies to hedge the ups and downs of my small, real-world, bond fund.

## Second

I continue to enjoy learning and developing my agent-based interpretations of [G&Ls](https://www.danielodriscoll.me.uk/reading#monetary-economics) monetary accounting systems. Some aspects of this stunningly powerful social system have become clearer over time. One particular enhancement I am keen to model is the opportunity for household agents to democratically allocate their taxation payments between a given collection of State infrastructure domains (for real-world similar, but not quite, see [UK Government Green Financing](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/uk-government-green-financing)). Of course, exceptional circumstances{{< super "__1__" >}} demand exceptional policies, but typically, new expenditure created by the Government and applied to each domain would be greater than - by an established (mutable) ratio - the amount of taxation payments marked by Households for a specific domain. Model Household agents, on average, inclined (categorical tax opinion setting?) to view taxation payments made to the Government as a *burden* (default setting) may by some measure switch to registering taxation payments as a *privilege* - perhaps promoting household agents to feel like active *club members* rather than dispirited *passive inmates*.

Domain specific infrastructure bonds (Consols) subsequently demanded by Households - and issued by Government - would each come with a fixed, low, immutable coupon rate (for real-world similar, but not quite, see [DMO Green Gilts Issuance](https://www.dmo.gov.uk/responsibilities/green-gilts/)). An individual policy interest rate would be applied to each new bond domain. Central Bank adjustments to one or more policy rates would seek to balance, as necessary, the investments made by Households via their asset portfolio calculations.

Examples{{< super "__1__" >}} :

- If Households mark a disproportionate amount to one specific domain only. (Model).
- In times of war and epidemic / pandemic. (Real-world input to the model).
